# Copyright-Free Books Added to Library

## Overview
Added 7 classic public domain horror/thriller stories to the library with copyright-free GIFs from Giphy.

## New Books Added

### 1. **The Tell-Tale Heart** by Edgar Allan Poe
- **Genre**: Horror
- **Cover GIF**: Creepy eye animation
- **Synopsis**: A murderer's guilt manifests as the relentless beating of his victim's heart
- **Why it's great**: Classic psychological horror, unreliable narrator, timeless guilt metaphor
- **Public Domain**: Published 1843

### 2. **The Yellow Wallpaper** by Charlotte Perkins Gilman
- **Genre**: Horror
- **Cover GIF**: Wallpaper pattern animation
- **Synopsis**: A woman's descent into madness, trapped in a room with sinister yellow wallpaper
- **Why it's great**: Feminist horror classic, realistic psychological breakdown, social commentary
- **Public Domain**: Published 1892

### 3. **The Masque of the Red Death** by Edgar Allan Poe
- **Genre**: Horror
- **Cover GIF**: Red/death themed animation
- **Synopsis**: A prince tries to escape a deadly plague by hiding in his abbey
- **Why it's great**: Rich symbolism, allegory of mortality, eerily relevant
- **Public Domain**: Published 1842

### 4. **The Monkey's Paw** by W. W. Jacobs
- **Genre**: Horror
- **Cover GIF**: Mysterious hand/paw animation
- **Synopsis**: A cursed monkey's paw grants three wishes, each with a terrible price
- **Why it's great**: Ultimate "be careful what you wish for" story, terrifying ending
- **Public Domain**: Published 1902

### 5. **The Cask of Amontillado** by Edgar Allan Poe
- **Genre**: Thriller
- **Cover GIF**: Dark catacombs/wine cellar animation
- **Synopsis**: A tale of cold, calculated revenge in the catacombs
- **Why it's great**: Perfect revenge thriller, atmospheric, unreliable narrator
- **Public Domain**: Published 1846

### 6. **The Pit and the Pendulum** by Edgar Allan Poe
- **Genre**: Horror
- **Cover GIF**: Pendulum/blade animation
- **Synopsis**: Trapped in a Spanish Inquisition torture chamber
- **Why it's great**: Visceral torture horror, survival thriller, historical context
- **Public Domain**: Published 1842

### 7. **The Signal-Man** by Charles Dickens
- **Genre**: Mystery
- **Cover GIF**: Train/railway themed animation
- **Synopsis**: A railway signal-man is haunted by a spectral figure that appears before disasters
- **Why it's great**: Victorian ghost story, premonition horror, industrial-age fear
- **Public Domain**: Published 1866
